History of WRC
How it started.

The World Rally Championship (abbreviated as WRC) is an international rallying series owned and governed by the FIA. Inaugurated in 1973, it is the second oldest of the FIA's world championships after Formula One.

More Info

Early Era

The 1973 World Rally Championship was the inaugural season of the WRC and began with the Monte Carlo Rally on 19 January.

Group B Era

The 1980s saw the rear-wheel-drive Group 2 and the more popular Group 4 cars be replaced by more powerful four-wheel-drive Group B cars.

Group A era

As the planned Group S was also cancelled, Group A regulations became the standard in the WRC until 1997. A separate Group A championship had been organized.

World Rally Car Era

For the 1997 World Rally Championship, the World Rally Car regulations were introduced as an intended replacement for Group A

Interesting Facts

World Rally Championship Fact

- 1911 The first official rally

The Monte Carlo Rally, which started in 1911, is widely considered the first official rally racing event. It has since become one of the most prestigious rallies in the world.

+ 120 It involves various types of stages.

Rally stages can vary in length and difficulty. They can range from short, fast-paced sprint stages to long endurance stages that test both the driver’s skills and the car’s reliability.

Starting first? No thanks!

Nobody wants to be first out on the road. That’s because the track is dirty with debris, dust and standing water – once a few hot rally cars have gone through and swept the road, it’s cleaner, grippier and easier to handle.

Are you a millionare?

Rallies aren't cheap. The sponsors’ cash is quickly spent on the road with the WRC. Wrecking your car can cost around €500,000. That’s just for the car, so don’t forget you also need to cover tyres, spare parts, fuel…

Feel the chill

Cars, drivers and teams have to be ready to deal with temperatures from 50ºC (like in Argentina) to -30ºC (at Rally Sweden). There’s no air-con, either – all the heating comes from the engine and the ventilation comes through the window…

Burning rubber

The weather can change in a matter of minutes, so the teams and the drivers need to have all the tyre options available at all times. That means 62 tyres per car, including soft, hard and intermediate compounds.

WRC Drivers

Greatest WRC Drivers

Walter Röhrl

Active Years: 1973 – 1987

Röhrl has scored 14 victories over his career, with his notable achievements including winning the World Rally Championship twice

Tommi Mäkinen

Active Years: 1987–2003

Mäkinen is one of the most successful WRC drivers of all time, ranking fifth in rally wins (24) and third in championships (4)

Juha Kankkunen

Active Years: 1979, 1982 – 2002, 2010

From 1983 to 2002, he secured 23 rally wins and four drivers' world titles, once series records in the World Rally Championship.

Sébastien Ogier

Active Years: 2008 - present

Sébastien Ogier has won the World Rally Drivers' Championship 8 times, in 2013, 2014, 2015, 2016, 2017, 2018, 2020, and 2021.

Sébastien Loeb

Active Years: 1999 - 2022

Sébastien Loeb holds the record for the most consecutive World Rally Championship titles, securing nine in a row.

Colin McRae

Active Years: 1973 – 1987

He was the 1991 and 1992 British Rally Champion, and in 1995 became the first British driver to win the World Rally Championship Drivers' title.

Calendar

In its 52nd season, the WRC will venture to 13 captivating destinations across four continents, delivering a perfect blend of tradition and innovation that aligns with rallying’s enduring legacy.

1911

Rallye Monte-Carlo

Rallye Monte-Carlo, the WRC's premier event and the oldest in its calendar, originated in 1911 as a promotional endeavor to showcase Monte-Carlo as a tourist haven. Commencing from diverse European cities, participants converged in Monaco, navigating unpredictable mountain weather. Despite its predominantly asphalt terrain, the rally poses challenges with ever-changing conditions, featuring snow, ice, and dry asphalt, often encountered within a single.

Monaco 3 Days Jan 25 - 28

1950

Rally Sweden

First held in 1950, Rally Sweden is one of the oldest rallies in the world. It is also the only rally on the WRC calendar to take place entirely on snow and ice, making it a unique and popular spectacle among fans worldwide. In pursuit of the ultimate winter conditions, the event moved north in 2022 to a new base in Umeå. Drivers ‘lean’ their cars against the banks to guide them round corners. But in warmer temperatures the banks disintegrate on impact and cars can become stuck in the snow.

Umeå 3 Days Feb 15 - 18

1953

Safari Rally Kenya

The Safari Rally was first held in 1953, as the East African Coronation Safari in Kenya, Uganda and Tanganyika as a celebration of the coronation of Queen Elizabeth II. In 1960 it was renamed the East African Safari Rally and kept that name until 1974, when it became the Kenya Safari Rally. The event adopted a special stage format in 1996 and from then until 2002, it featured over 1000km of timed tests. The rally left the WRC in 2002 and returned in 2021.

Nairobi 3 Days Mar 28 - 31

2021

Croatia Rally

A newcomer to the WRC in 2021, Croatia Rally has proved itself to be huge challenge with ever-changing grip levels and surface conditions characterising the first world-level rally in Europe’s eastern region since Rally Bulgaria in 2010. The sealed surface special stages are slippery even in the dry, while the asphalt varies from super-smooth via worn to badly broken. Throw in crests and jumps, and it’s easy to see why this third round is a favourite among the drivers.

Zagreb 3 Days Apr 18 - 21

1973

Rally De Portugal

A founding round of the WRC back in 1973, Rally de Portugal is ranked as one of the WRC's classic events with challenging gravel stages to the east and south of Porto. Varying road surfaces and changeable weather add to the task facing crews. It’s also extremely popular among fans with enthusiastic spectators following the action in their tens of thousands. Rally de Portugal has been awarded "The Best Rally in the World" five times and in 2000 "The Most Improved Rally of the Year".

Matosinhos 3 Days May 9 - 12

1973

Rally Italia Sardegna

One of the founding rounds from the championship’s beginning in 1973, Italy’s WRC counter was formerly a mixed surface event based in Sanremo before it moved to the Mediterranean island of Sardinia in 2004. Fast but narrow stages lined with bushes, trees or rocks on the edge leave no room for error, while the hard-base roads are covered by a sandy surface which is swept away during the first pass to leave rougher and rutted conditions for the second run.

Alghero 3 Days May 30 - Jun 2

1921

Rally Poland

The Rally of Poland is a motorsport event for rally cars that was first established in 1921. It is third-oldest rally in the world, preceded only by Österreichische Alpenfahrt and Monte Carlo Rally. The event became a permanent fixture of the European Rally Championship in 1960, except for a few editions held as part of the World Rally Championship.

Mikolajki 3 Days Jun 27 - 30

2013

Rally Latvia

The rally was run for the first time in 2013 as the first international auto racing event to be held in Latvia since gaining independence from the Soviet Union in 1991 and just the second motorsport event after the motorcycle Speedway Grand Prix of Latvia. In 2016 the rally was moved to a September date, becoming a gravel rally, before settling into October in 2017.

Liepāja 3 Days Jul 18 - 21

1951

Rally Finland

A mix of hard, wide roads with narrower more technical sections. Flat out from start to finish. It is affectionately known as the Finnish Grand Prix and nine of the 10 fastest WRC rallies have been here. Pace notes must be perfect. Blind crests often hide corners and it’s vital to position the car correctly before take-off to ensure maximum speed through the bend.

Jyväskylä 3 Days Aug 1 - 4

1953

Acropolis Rally Greece

Hard base roads and high temperatures require careful management of tyres and cars. Conditions are especially tricky on repeated stages, when rocks and bedrock unearthed during the first pass can be especially damaging. Avoid mechanical issues. An opening leg with no service and long morning and afternoon sections on Saturday mean technical troubles will be severely punished. The event was based in Lamia in Central Greece.

Athens 3 Days Sep 5 - 8

2018

Rally Chile

A relative newcomer to the WRC, Rally Chile Bio Bío was first held in 2018 when it ran as a candidate event as part of a bid to join the WRC calendar. Its success meant Chile was added to the WRC calendar for 2019 - at the time becoming the 32nd country to host a WRC round since the championship's inception in 1973. Ott Tänak, driving a Toyota Yaris World Rally Car, claimed victory by 23.1 seconds over Sébastien Ogier.

Concepción 3 Days Sep 26 - 29

2023

Central European Rally

The Central European Rally is a European rally competition that debuted in 2023 as part of the World Rally Championship, co-hosted between Germany, Austria and Czech Republic.[1] The rally is based in southeast Germany, in the city of Passau in Bavaria, and is run on tarmac. The event was based in Passau, Bavaria, Germany, and contested over eighteen special stages covering a total competitive distance of 311.79 km (193.74 mi).

Passau 3 Days Oct 31 - Nov 3

2004

Rally Japan

Rally Japan first featured in the WRC in 2004 and was held, up until 2007, in Hokkaido’s Tokachi-Obihiro area. In 2008 and 2010 (there was no rally in 2009) the event was based in Central Hokkaido, with Sapporo as the host town. Subaru’s Peter Solberg won the inaugural rally in 2004. In 2005, the winner was Peugeot’s Marcus Grönholm, and the 2006 winner was Sébastien Loeb, with Citroën.

Nagoya 3 Days Nov 21 - 24
